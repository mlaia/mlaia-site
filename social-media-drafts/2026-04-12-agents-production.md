## LinkedIn Company Post (150–200 words)

Most enterprise AI agent pilots never reach production.

Industry estimates suggest roughly **74% of agentic AI projects stall** before sustained deployment — not because the technology doesn't work, but because teams underestimate what production actually requires.

We published a practical framework today covering the four pillars that determine whether an agent deployment succeeds:

1. **Scope Design** — defining exactly what the agent can and cannot do before a single prompt is written
2. **Tool & Memory Architecture** — idempotent tools, typed schemas, structured errors, retrieval layers
3. **Human-in-the-Loop Patterns** — approval gates, shadow mode, confidence-gated autonomy
4. **Observability** — trace logging, drift detection, and immutable audit trails for regulated industries

The article also covers Israeli-specific realities that most vendor frameworks miss: data residency constraints, Hebrew/English multilingual evaluation, and the talent market for production AI engineers.

If you are navigating an agent deployment — or trying to rescue a pilot that stalled — this is a framework worth reading.
